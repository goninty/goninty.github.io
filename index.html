About Me

My name's Andrew, I'm a final year Computer Science student at the University of Bath.

I am proficient in:

  • C#, used during development of my final year project.
  • Java, building both command line applications and GUI-based programs with Swing.
  • Python, writing scripts to bring personal and hackathon projects to life.
  • Git and GitHub - used for individual projects, group courseworks and hackathon projects.

I have some experience in:

  • C and C++ used throughout the development of several pieces of coursework.
  • VB.Net for creating GUI-based Windows form applications at A-Level.
  • JavaScript - using both React.js and jQuery to build dynamic websites.
  • Android Studio for building an app as a piece of university coursework.
  • HTML and CSS.

At the University of Bath, I've held a few roles.

In my second year, I was a PAL (Peer Assisted Learning) Leader. I helped host weekly sessions for first years in which we went over lecture content, answered questions and provided coursework help. You can find out more about the university's PAL schemes here.

In my final year, I was a Senior PAL Leader. Each week, I held debrief meetings with the new PAL Leaders. We chatted about how their sessions were going and worked together on diagnosing issues and planning future sessions. I also met weekly with the Peer Support department of my university's SU - sharing ideas with Senior PAL Leaders from a wide range of different departments.

I was also an Academic Rep in my second year. I communicated with other students in my year to elicit any concerns, questions or problems they had regarding our course. Whether it was coursework, the lectures themselves or anything else - no matter how big or small. I attended regular meetings with other reps and staff to discuss these issues and try to get them resolved.

In my spare time I am an avid gamer as well as a PC hardware enthusiast.
